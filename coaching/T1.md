# Coaching Note

## Exact Agent Quote
"I understand. Our payments team typically resolves this within 24 hours."

## What the Agent Said
In this pivot turn, the agent acknowledges the user's issue by saying they understand; however, they add that the payments team typically resolves it within 24 hours. The tone is somewhat neutral and lacks urgency. While the agent offers some assurance, the phrasing may come off as dismissive given the user's heightened frustration. This does not address the immediate concern of the user who is facing a significant delay affecting their trading activities.

## What the Agent Should Have Said
"I completely understand how frustrating this situation must be, especially since you need to trade urgently. I will escalate this to our payments team right away and keep you updated on the progress. Can I also assist you with anything else in the meantime?"

## Missed Coaching Technique
**Empathy with Resolution**: This technique focuses on recognizing the customer's feelings and providing reassurance while actively engaging in a resolution process. By acknowledging the customer's frustration and committing to make things right, the agent helps to create a connection that is both empathetic and solution-oriented.

## Why the Alternative Reduces Escalation
The improved response would likely de-escalate the customer’s frustration by first validating their feelings of urgency and annoyance. By expressing an understanding of their frustration and committing to escalate the matter, the agent conveys a sense of urgency and care for the user's situation. This proactive engagement can help the customer feel heard and valued, thus reducing their negative sentiment and likelihood of escalating the matter further. By also offering to assist with any other inquiries, the agent demonstrates a willingness to help beyond the immediate concern, which enhances customer satisfaction and trust.
